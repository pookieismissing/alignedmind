Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers about care, policies, and what to expect

You may begin by contacting the practice to request an initial psychiatric evaluation. During the first appointment, your history, concerns, and goals will be reviewed to determine whether services offered here are an appropriate fit.

This is a self pay practice and does not accept insurance, including Medicare or Medicaid. Payment is due at the time of service. Clients may request a receipt or superbill for personal records or possible out of network reimbursement.

All services are provided in person at the Connecticut practice location. Telehealth and virtual appointments are not offered at this time.

This practice provides outpatient psychiatric evaluation and medication management for individuals whose needs can be safely managed in a low acuity setting.

Medication may be prescribed when clinically appropriate. Controlled substances are not prescribed under any circumstances. All treatment decisions are made carefully and collaboratively.

Initial psychiatric evaluations and follow up appointments vary in length depending on clinical need. Appointment details and fees are discussed in advance.

At least twenty four hours notice is required to cancel or reschedule an appointment. Late cancellations or missed appointments may result in a seventy five dollar fee.

Information shared during treatment is confidential and protected by federal and Connecticut law. There are legal limits to confidentiality which will be reviewed during care.

This practice does not provide emergency services. If you are experiencing a psychiatric emergency, call 911, go to the nearest emergency department, or call or text 988.
